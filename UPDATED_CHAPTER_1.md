**CHAPTER 1**

**BACKGROUND OF THE STUDY**

**Introduction**

In the modern educational landscape, the efficiency of administrative processes is crucial to the success of an institution and the satisfaction of its students. Yllana Bay View College faces the challenge of managing a large volume of student data, subject offerings, academic records, and financial ledgers.

Currently, many processes such as subject searching, grade tracking, attendance monitoring, and permit issuance are handled through semi-manual or fragmented digital systems. This often leads to delays, difficulty in tracking student progress, and potential errors in academic reporting. Students frequently experience long wait times, while administrative staff spend excessive time searching through physical ledgers or disconnected spreadsheets to verify records and process payments.

To address these challenges, the proponents developed the Student Management System. This system is designed to centralize academic data, automate grade recording and attendance, and streamline the mass issuance of student permits and semester-based tuition monitoring. By transitioning to a unified web-based platform, the college can ensure faster, more accurate, and more reliable services for its entire academic community.

**Project Context**

The existing manual and semi-digital methods serve as a significant bottleneck to institutional efficiency. The proposed Student Management System aims to eliminate these drawbacks by providing a real-time, centralized database for all student-related transactions.

This study will assist the organization in providing an accurate and satisfying experience for students and staff alike. The system will allow for easy tracking of subject schedules, academic performance, and semester-based financial balances. If implemented, it will drastically reduce paper usage, minimize the risk of data loss, and allow the administration to generate detailed reports—such as printable student ledgers and automated bulk permit assignments—in seconds rather than hours.

**Purpose & Description of the Study**

The Student Management System provides a secure and reliable platform to handle core academic operations. It replaces time-consuming manual lookups with a fast, search-optimized interface secured by Role-Based Access Control.

**Beneficiaries of the system include:**

*   **Students:** Can easily view their own grades, subject schedules filtered by semester, attendance, ledger records, and permit status without visiting multiple offices.
*   **Teachers:** Can efficiently manage student lists and track daily attendance automatically based on enrolled subjects.
*   **Registrar:** Gains a centralized tool to manage subject offerings, standardize schedules, and oversee academic records.
*   **SAPS (Student Affairs/Student Programs):** Can automatically assign and manage permit numbers in bulk for eligible students.
*   **Cashier:** Can record over-the-counter payments, monitor semester-based student balances with accuracy, and generate ledger booklets.


**Significance Of The Study**

The Student Management System holds significant value as it addresses the long-standing challenges of manual academic record-keeping. The successful implementation of this study will benefit the following:

*   **To the Students:** It provides them with a faster and more transparent way to view their grades, financial ledgers, and secure their exam permits. This reduces the frustration of waiting in long queues.
*   **To the Teachers:** It simplifies the complex task of grade computation and attendance tracking. Teachers can focus more on student engagement rather than spending hours on manual data entry.
*   **To the Registrar's Office:** It streamlines subject management and enrollment monitoring. The system minimizes human error in recording subjects and schedules, ensuring data remains accurate.
*   **To the SAPS and Cashier Offices:** It provides an efficient way to track student permits and tuition balances per semester, reducing administrative workload through automated ledger printing and one-click bulk permit issuance.
*   **To the School Administration:** It enhances the overall reputation of Yllana Bay View College by professionalizing its student services and administrative operations through modern technology and secure access.
*   **To Future Researchers:** This study will serve as a valuable reference and guide for other IT students conducting research on student management systems.

**Objectives**

The primary objective of this study is to develop a Student Management System for Yllana Bay View College to automate academic recording and improve the efficiency of student services.

**Specific Objectives:**

1.  To create a centralized database for student profiles, subject offerings, grade records, and attendance tracking.
2.  To implement a Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) system to ensure data security and privacy across different departments (Admin, Registrar, Cashier, SAPS, Teachers, Students).
3.  To develop an automated process for mass-assigning student permits and tracking semester-based tuition balances.
4.  To build a dynamic Class Attendance Management system featuring spreadsheet-style tracking and automated Excel exportation.
5.  To provide a user-friendly interface with standardized scheduling options for searching subjects and viewing academic performance per semester.
6.  To evaluate the system's performance in terms of accuracy, reliability, and user satisfaction.

**Scope and Limitation**

**Scope**

The scope of this system encompasses a comprehensive suite of academic and administrative management features. It features a robust user management module that provides secure login with Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), ensuring that students, teachers, the registrar, cashiers, SAPS, and administrators have specific access tailored to their roles. The system handles subject tracking by allowing users to manage standardized schedules, assigned rooms, and professors. For academic performance, the grade management module enables the seamless inputting, computing, and viewing of student grades. Additionally, the system incorporates a teacher-centric attendance management interface with automated generation of spreadsheet reports. Administrative processes are supported by an automated permit system capable of bulk-issuing examination permit numbers to eligible students. Lastly, the financial aspect is covered by the student ledger module, which tracks semester-based tuition, facilitates the printing of receipts, and generates complete ledger booklets.

**Limitation**

Despite its extensive features, the system has certain limitations that must be acknowledged. Notably, it does not support online payment gateways; therefore, all financial transactions and payments must still be settled physically over-the-counter at the cashier’s office. Additionally, it is a web-responsive platform accessed via a browser, lacking a dedicated mobile application for iOS or Android devices. Its operation is dependent on network availability, requiring an active internet connection. Finally, the system does not include automated SMS capabilities; it cannot send automatic text message notifications for impending dues, attendance alerts, or updates regarding student grades.

**Definition Of Terms**

*   **SMS (Student Management System):** The software application used to manage student data and academic processes.
*   **SAPS:** Student Affairs/Student Programs; the office responsible for student welfare and permit issuance.
*   **Permit Number:** A unique code assigned to students allowing them to take major examinations.
*   **GPA (Grade Point Average):** The calculated average of a student's grades used to measure academic performance.
*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** A security method that restricts system access based on the user's role (e.g., student, teacher, cashier).
*   **Tuition Balance:** The remaining amount a student needs to pay for their school fees for a specific semester.
*   **Student Ledger Booklet:** A generated, printable document that records a student's complete financial assessment and payment history in a professional layout.
*   **Bulk Permit Assignment:** A feature that allows SAPS to automatically issue exam permits to all financially eligible students with a single click.
